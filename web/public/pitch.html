The problem

A trillion-dollar habit that runs on a notebook

Rotating savings circles — sandoq, esusu, chit funds, tandas — let a group save together: everyone pays in each round, the whole pot goes to one member, rotating until all are paid.

  • The organizer holds every contribution — and sometimes disappears with it.
  • A member who collects early stops paying.
  • Records live in one person's head. Disputes have no referee.
  • It can't cross a border — diaspora families are shut out.
The solution

Move the trust into a smart contract

Sandoq keeps the social circle and removes its failure modes. A Soroban contract — not a person — escrows every stake and pays out every pot.

  • Nobody holds the money. Not the organizer, not us.
  • Miss a round? It's covered from your own collateral first.
  • Payouts are a permissionless crank — the circle finishes itself.
  • Every deposit and payout is public and verifiable on-chain.
How it works

Fill → contribute → settle → rotate

Filling   each member stakes collateral to take a seat →
Active    every round: everyone contributes, the pot pays one member →
         a missed round is covered from that member's stake →
Complete every member has been paid once; stakes come back.

The contract ends holding exactly zero — an invariant checked after every settle in the tests.

Why it's safe

Collateral is a dial, not a leap of faith

Sandoq doesn't manufacture trust between strangers — it removes the need for a trusted organizer, and lets each circle choose how much trust the rest requires.

0×
Trusted friends — a missed round just shrinks the pot
1×
A community — covers an occasional miss
(n−1)×
Strangers — fully trustless, even if one takes the pot and runs
Why Stellar

A payments chain, with the last mile built in

  • ~5s finality, near-zero fees — small monthly contributions actually make sense.
  • Anchors (SEP-24) — members enter and exit in local currency; the wallet-to-cash link informal savers need.
  • Soroban — escrow, a factory that deploys one contract per circle, cross-contract aggregation.
  • Settles in a stable asset, so no member carries volatility risk.
